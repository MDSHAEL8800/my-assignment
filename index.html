Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Driving economic growth and societal advancement through technology, research, and entrepreneurship. This pillar emphasizes fostering a culture of creativity, digital transformation, and investment in cutting-edge industries such as AI, green energy, and biotechnology.

Sustainability

Ensuring that growth aligns with environmental stewardship and resource efficiency. Sustainability involves adopting renewable energy, promoting circular economy practices, and mitigating climate risks. The aim is to balance economic development with ecological preservation.

Infrastructure

Building robust, modern, and resilient infrastructure to connect people, industries, and markets. This includes advancements in transport networks, smart cities, digital infrastructure, and renewable energy grids Prioritizing infrastructure modernization accelerates industrial growth and international trade.

Empowerment

Focusing on the upliftment and inclusion of all demographics, especially women, youth, and marginalized communities. Empowerment involves promoting education, skills development, and entrepreneurship while ensuring equitable access to opportunities and resources.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nations trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

Karnaphuli Tunnel: Bangladesh's First Underwater Expressway

The Karnaphuli Tunnel, also known as the Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman Tunnel, is South Asia’s first underwater tunnel. Located in Chattogram, it connects the city’s two banks beneath the Karnaphuli River.
